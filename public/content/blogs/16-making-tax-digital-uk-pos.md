# Making Tax Digital Is Coming for Every UK Business — Is Your POS Ready?

**HMRC's Making Tax Digital programme has been expanding since 2019. If your POS system can't produce compliant VAT records, you are already behind.**

---

## What Making Tax Digital Actually Requires

Making Tax Digital (MTD) is HMRC's programme to move UK business tax record-keeping and reporting onto digital systems. The requirements are not optional for businesses above the relevant thresholds — they are law.

For VAT, the timeline has been clear:

- April 2019: MTD for VAT mandated for businesses with taxable turnover above £85,000
- April 2022: MTD for VAT extended to all VAT-registered businesses, regardless of turnover

That second date is the one that catches people. Since April 2022, every VAT-registered business in the UK — including one-person operations and businesses with turnover well below the registration threshold who chose to register voluntarily — must keep digital VAT records and submit VAT returns through MTD-compatible software.

MTD for Income Tax Self Assessment (ITSA) is next. From April 2026, sole traders and landlords with qualifying income above £50,000 must submit quarterly updates to HMRC digitally. The £30,000 threshold follows in April 2027.

The direction of travel is clear: every aspect of UK business tax reporting is moving to digital, mandated submission. The question is not whether your software needs to be MTD-compatible. It's whether it already is.

## The Compliance Gap in Most POS Systems

Most point-of-sale systems capture transaction data. Most can export sales reports. These are not the same as MTD compliance.

MTD compliance requires:
1. Digital records kept in a compatible format from the point of transaction
2. VAT return data submitted directly from that software to HMRC, via HMRC's API (not manually re-entered)
3. A digital audit trail that connects each transaction to the VAT return

Square, for example, lets you export transaction data as a CSV. You then either manually transfer those numbers into MTD-compatible accounting software (Xero, QuickBooks, FreeAgent) or pay an accountant to do it. The POS itself does not submit to HMRC. The connection between point of sale and MTD submission requires an additional step — and that step costs time or money every quarter.

For a business owner who is already managing operations, stock, staff, and customers, adding quarterly compliance work to the list is not a minor inconvenience. It's the kind of thing that gets done late, or gets done wrong, or gets outsourced at £50–150 per VAT return to an accountant.

## The Audit Trail Requirement

HMRC doesn't just want your VAT totals. Under MTD, you're required to maintain digital records that can be inspected if HMRC chooses to investigate a return. Those records need to show, at minimum: the time of supply, the value of the supply, and the VAT rate applied.

A VAT investigation — even a routine one — typically asks for evidence behind the numbers. If your records consist of a CSV export and a pile of manual adjustments made in a spreadsheet, the process of responding to HMRC is labour-intensive and expensive.

AskBiz maintains a complete audit trail: every transaction, every refund, every void, every staff action, timestamped and logged. If HMRC asks, you can produce the records — by date range, by transaction type, by staff member — without manual compilation.

This audit trail satisfies HMRC requirements and also has operational value independent of compliance: it's how you investigate discrepancies, verify staff transactions, and track the performance of specific periods. The compliance investment and the operational investment are the same system.

## What Happens When You Get It Wrong

HMRC's approach to MTD non-compliance has moved through a period of relatively light-touch enforcement into firmer territory. The penalty regime for MTD failures includes points-based penalties for late submissions and financial penalties for persistent non-compliance.

More practically: if you're not keeping digital records as required and you're selected for a VAT inspection, the remediation costs — accountant time to reconstruct records, possible penalties for record-keeping failures — can substantially exceed what you'd have paid to use compliant software from the start.

HMRC also has the ability to cross-reference payment processor data with submitted VAT returns. If your card payment totals from your payment processor don't reconcile with your submitted VAT figures, that discrepancy is potentially visible to HMRC without them needing to contact you at all. The message is: the numbers need to be right, and you need to be able to show your working.

## VAT on Mixed Supplies

For businesses covering multiple sectors — a café selling food (zero-rated) and beverages (standard-rated), or a repair shop selling parts (standard-rated) and labour (standard-rated, though at different margins) — VAT accounting gets more complex. Different VAT rates apply to different parts of the transaction, and the split needs to be recorded accurately at the point of sale.

AskBiz handles VAT rate assignment at product/service level. When a café serves a customer a coffee (20% VAT) and a cold sandwich (zero-rated), the transaction records both elements at the correct rate. The VAT return accurately reflects the mix. No manual adjustment needed.

For a multi-sector business — the garage that also runs a retail counter, or the salon that also sells products — this per-item VAT accuracy across sectors is particularly important. Getting it wrong isn't just a compliance risk; it's a cash flow issue if you're either over-reporting and paying more VAT than you owe, or under-reporting and accumulating a liability.

## The MTD ITSA Horizon

VAT is only the beginning. MTD for Income Tax Self Assessment starts in April 2026, requiring quarterly digital reporting of business income and expenses for sole traders and landlords above the relevant thresholds.

A POS system that accurately captures business income throughout the year — with the transaction data that an ITSA quarterly submission requires — dramatically reduces the work involved in that reporting. If your POS is capturing every transaction accurately, your quarterly ITSA update is largely derived from data that already exists, not assembled from scratch.

The UK business tax environment is moving, unambiguously and legislatively, toward digital-first real-time reporting. A POS system that is built with that in mind is not just more convenient — it's the right infrastructure for the regulatory environment you're operating in.
